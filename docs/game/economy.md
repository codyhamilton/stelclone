# Resource Economy and Financial Systems

## Mineral Types and Rarity

The sector contains three tiers of specialized minerals, each with unique properties and economic value. Note that basic construction materials (iron, copper, aluminum, etc.) are broadly available throughout human space and are considered "junk" materials - they are not the focus of the game's resource economy.

### Frontier Minerals (Common, Random None To High Initial Yield)
Each one has a 50% chance to appear on any asteroid
- **Cobaltite**: Essential for advanced electronics
- **Vanadium**: Critical for high-temperature alloys
- **Chromium**: Used in advanced structural materials
- **Molybdenum**: Key component in fusion reactor components

### Exotic Minerals (Uncommon, Random None to Variable Yield)
Each one has a chance to appear on a specific asteroid
- **Rhenium**: Advanced propulsion systems (40%)
- **Osmium**: High-density structural applications (40%)
- **Rhodium**: Advanced catalytic systems (20%)
- **Iridium**: Premium industrial applications (20%)

### Xenon Minerals (Fixed Supply)
Appear on small proportion (10%) of asteroids
- **Helium-42**: High energy unstable material opening highly advanced technology requiring order-of-magnitude more energy than previously available
- **Crystal Matrix**: Exotic material for advanced technology, used for previously-unattainable computational power

## Mining Mechanics

### Resource Availability
- Each asteroid has a fixed, limited supply of rare minerals
- Once depleted, rare minerals cannot be mined from that asteroid
- Low and high grade minerals follow a half-life yield system:
  - Initial yield density varies by mineral type
  - Each mining cycle reduces remaining density by 50%
  - A mining cycle is when an amount of ore equivalent to that which a single mine building will take in a year
  - Low grade minerals typically start with medium to high density (50-100%)
  - High grade minerals have variable starting density (20-100%)
  - Yield decreases exponentially over time

### Mining Operations
- Mining requires a colony with dedicated Mine buildings
- Resources are stored in Storage buildings on the asteroid
- Storage capacity must be managed to prevent production bottlenecks
- Multiple Mine buildings can operate simultaneously
- Mining efficiency affected by:
  - Current resource density
  - Number of Mine buildings
  - Storage capacity
  - Worker efficiency

## Economic Systems

### Currency and Trading
- **Credits**: Primary currency for all transactions
- **Resource Trading**: 
  - Physical trading requires ships present at asteroid locations
  - Resources must be stored at the trading location
  - Multiple trading options available:
    - Galactic Resource Freighters (infrequent, fixed prices)
    - On-demand traders (negotiable prices, unreliable availability)
    - Direct trading with alien civilizations
  - Trading prices influenced by:
    - Current market conditions
    - Distance to trading partner
    - Resource scarcity
    - Diplomatic relations

### Operational Costs
- **Worker Management**:
  - All workers must be paid regular wages
  - Wage levels affect worker efficiency and satisfaction
  - Workers may only arrive or leave on corporate freighters which arrive at specific times
  - Labor union negotiations and strikes
  - Dead or fired workers cost for repatriation to their home
  - Dead workers have a "widows fund" cost for 10 years
  - Fired workers have a termination payment

- **Infrastructure Upkeep**:
  - Building maintenance, fixed amount per building type
  - Repairs, cost per HP repaired
  - Building maintenance is reduced 80% while a building is offline
  - Power maintenance is 50% fixed and rest scales with power usage

- **Fleet Maintenance**:
  - Ship repair and maintenance
  - Ships maintenance reduced 50% while docked, or 10% if put in storage (minimal maintenance staff)
  - Staff costs fixed unless the ships are in storage, but getting staff back takes time

### Financial Management
- **Investment Options**:
  - Bank loans with varying interest rates
  - Stock market trading of company shares

- **Shareholder System**:
  - Issue new shares to raise capital
  - Shareholders receive percentage of profits
  - Dividend payments
