# Missiles

## Philosophy

Missiles represent the primary long-range standoff capability in space combat. Unlike direct-fire weapons that require line-of-sight and immediate engagement, missiles provide strategic depth to combat encounters. They allow players to engage targets at extended ranges, forcing opponents to invest in defensive systems or risk taking significant damage.

In a strategic sense, missiles provide a kind of domain of dominance for players. They are effective within a specific range of their silo, do not require upkeep and are substantially cheaper to produce than ships. In effect they provide a static barrier to encroachment by enemies around established colonies.

If a player has a well stocked inventory they can effectively block sustainable colonisation in a wide area around their asteroid, forcing the competition to deal with their colony first. This can be used for containment, to protect a valuable field of resources or to protect deeper asteroids from enemy bombardment (keeping them at range)

Since it takes time to establish a colony and build effective defensive systems on it, doing so within the range of an established colony requires a large fleet to maintain point defense for an extended period. This can be used to overcome overwhelming missile dominance, but it requires long term fleet commitment and risks enemy attacking the protecting fleet.

Key Characteristics
 - Require credits and resources to construct but require no upkeep
 - Require missile silos to build, store, and launch
 - Large quantities can be stocked
 - Only effective against asteroid colonies

## Categories

Missiles are divided into two primary categories:

### Basic Missiles
Available from the start of the game, these represent standard military hardware that any faction can produce. 

Characteristics:
- Lack specialised capabilities
- Mostly require only basic materials
- On the cheaper side to produce in cost
- Variations trade off cost vs range vs speed vs damage

### Advanced Missiles
Require blueprints obtained through exploration, trade, or corporate relationships. These represent cutting-edge technology developed by major corporations during the Corporate Wars. Each advanced missile type offers unique tactical advantages that can dramatically alter combat dynamics. Obtaining these blueprints represents a significant strategic milestone, as they unlock new combat capabilities unavailable to those relying solely on basic technology.

Advanced missile advantages might include:
- Faster speeds
- Higher damage
- Avoidance of ship-based point defense
- Alternative payloads (EMP, Virus, Ground penetratation (destroys building capable land))
- Specific building targeting


## Construction

Missile production requires a combination of credits and strategic resources. The resource requirements create economic pressure on players, forcing them to balance missile stockpiles against other ship construction and upgrade needs. More powerful missiles require rarer resources, creating a natural progression gate that prevents players from immediately fielding overwhelming firepower.

Advanced missiles require significant blueprint investments, representing the cost of acquiring proprietary technology. These blueprints are one-time purchases that unlock permanent production capabilities, making them valuable long-term investments for players focused on military expansion.

## Types

### Basic Missiles

#### Thunderbolt
**Description**
The workhorse of colony bombardment, the Thunderbolt represents standard military-grade missile technology. It provides reliable damage to colony infrastructure at moderate range, making it suitable for establishing defensive perimeters around your colonies.

**Background**
Developed as a standardized munition across multiple factions, the Thunderbolt's design prioritizes reliability and cost-effectiveness over specialized capabilities. Its conventional warhead and straightforward guidance systems make it easy to produce and maintain, perfect for stockpiling in large quantities to create defensive zones.

**Characteristics**
- Balanced damage and range
- Moderate speed
- Low resource requirements
- Cost-effective for area denial
- Standard colony infrastructure damage

#### Hellfire
**Description**
An enhanced variant that sacrifices range for increased destructive power. The Hellfire carries a larger explosive payload, making it effective for concentrated strikes against well-defended colonies or when maximum damage per missile is desired.

**Background**
Developed as an answer to increasingly fortified colony designs, the Hellfire represents an evolution of basic missile technology focused on raw destructive capability. When you need to ensure a colony cannot be established, the Hellfire delivers decisive damage.

**Characteristics**
- High damage output to colony infrastructure
- Shorter effective range
- Moderate speed
- Requires additional resources (Chromium)
- Effective for breaking through defensive systems

#### Lightning Strike
**Description**
A high-speed missile designed for rapid response to colony establishment attempts. While it carries a smaller warhead, its exceptional velocity makes it difficult for point-defense systems to intercept, allowing it to strike colonies before they can fully establish defensive infrastructure.

**Background**
Created to counter the critical window when colonies are most vulnerable during establishment, the Lightning Strike emphasizes speed over payload. It represents a tactical philosophy focused on rapid area denial and preventing enemy footholds.

**Characteristics**
- Very high speed (harder to intercept)
- Lower damage per missile
- Short range
- Excellent for rapid response to new colony attempts
- Cost-effective for maintaining constant threat

### Advanced Missiles

#### Dragon's Fang (Nova Dynamics)
**Description**
A specialized penetration missile that can bypass colony defensive structures through advanced warhead technology. Designed specifically to counter heavily fortified colonies, the dual-stage warhead first breaches defensive installations before delivering the main payload to critical infrastructure.

**Background**
Developed during the Corporate Wars, this missile was Nova Dynamics' answer to increasingly sophisticated colony fortifications. The dual-stage warhead system represents a significant technological advancement, allowing it to penetrate defensive layers that would stop conventional missiles.

**Characteristics**
- Penetrates colony defensive structures
- High damage to critical infrastructure
- Extended range
- Requires rare resources (Molybdenum)
- Blueprint investment required
- Effective against fortified colonies

#### Hydra (Nova Dynamics)
**Description**
A cluster missile that splits into multiple sub-munitions mid-flight, overwhelming colony point-defense systems. The sub-munitions can either saturate a single target area or spread across multiple colony structures, making it extremely difficult to defend against.

**Background**
Developed as a counter to increasingly sophisticated colony point-defense networks, the Hydra represents Nova Dynamics' approach to missile saturation tactics. Its ability to split into coordinated sub-munitions makes it particularly effective against defensive installations, forcing colonies to invest heavily in multiple defensive systems.

**Characteristics**
- Multi-warhead system
- Overwhelms point-defense installations
- Can damage multiple colony structures simultaneously
- High resource cost
- Blueprint investment required
- Forces expensive defensive investments

#### Leviathan (Galactic Industries)
**Description**
A long-range strategic missile designed for extended-range colony bombardment. Its massive payload and extreme range make it ideal for establishing dominance over vast areas, allowing a single colony to threaten enemy positions far beyond normal missile range.

**Background**
Originally developed for asteroid mining operations, Galactic Industries adapted this technology for military applications. The missile's ability to maintain high velocity over extreme distances represents a different engineering philosophy focused on strategic reach and area control.

**Characteristics**
- Extreme range (expands defensive perimeter significantly)
- Massive damage to colony infrastructure
- Slower speed
- Very high resource cost (including Rhenium)
- Blueprint investment required
- Creates extended zone of control

#### Ghost in the Machine (Galactic Industries)
**Description**
An electronic warfare missile that disables colony systems rather than causing direct structural damage. The EMP warhead temporarily shuts down colony infrastructure, preventing resource extraction, production, and defensive systems from operating. This creates windows of vulnerability without destroying the colony outright.

**Background**
Developed as a non-destructive alternative for corporate disputes, this EMP-based weapon became valuable for disabling enemy colonies without destroying them. Particularly effective against colonies with advanced electronic systems and automated infrastructure.

**Characteristics**
- Disables colony systems (production, extraction, defenses)
- Non-destructive but creates vulnerability windows
- Moderate range and speed
- High resource cost
- Blueprint investment required
- Forces temporary colony shutdown

#### Bunker Buster (Nova Dynamics)
**Description**
A ground-penetration missile designed to destroy building-capable terrain on asteroid surfaces. By eliminating the foundation space available for construction, this missile can permanently reduce a colony's maximum capacity and strategic value.

**Background**
Developed during territorial disputes, the Bunker Buster was designed to make captured colonies less valuable to enemies. By destroying the terrain itself, it ensures that even if a colony survives the attack, it cannot be fully rebuilt to its previous potential.

**Characteristics**
- Destroys building-capable land
- Permanently reduces colony capacity
- Moderate damage to existing structures
- High resource cost
- Blueprint investment required
- Strategic terrain denial weapon

#### Scorched Earth (Galactic Industries)
**Description**
A specialized missile that targets resource extraction infrastructure. Rather than destroying the colony itself, it focuses on mining operations, refineries, and processing facilities, crippling the colony's economic viability.

**Background**
Developed as an economic warfare tool, the Scorched Earth missile represents Galactic Industries' philosophy of winning through resource denial. By destroying extraction capabilities, it forces enemies to either abandon the colony or invest heavily in rebuilding economic infrastructure.

**Characteristics**
- Targets resource extraction buildings specifically
- Reduces colony economic output
- Lower overall damage but high strategic impact
- Moderate resource cost
- Blueprint investment required
- Economic warfare focus

#### Siege Breaker (Nova Dynamics)
**Description**
A missile specifically designed to target defensive installations. It carries specialized warheads optimized for destroying point-defense systems, shield generators, and missile silos, clearing the way for follow-up strikes.

**Background**
Developed to break through heavily defended colonies, the Siege Breaker represents Nova Dynamics' approach to overcoming defensive superiority. By eliminating defensive systems first, it makes subsequent missile strikes far more effective.

**Characteristics**
- Specifically targets defensive structures
- High damage to point-defense and shields
- Moderate range and speed
- High resource cost
- Blueprint investment required
- Opens colonies to follow-up attacks

#### Contamination Warhead (Galactic Industries)
**Description**
A missile that delivers a radioactive or toxic payload, creating an area of environmental hazard around the target colony. While not immediately destructive, it prevents safe operation of the colony for an extended period, effectively creating temporary area denial.

**Background**
Developed as a long-term area denial weapon, the Contamination Warhead represents a different approach to colony control. Rather than destroying infrastructure, it makes the area uninhabitable, forcing evacuation and preventing re-establishment until the contamination clears.

**Characteristics**
- Creates environmental hazard zone
- Prevents colony operation temporarily
- Long-lasting area denial effect
- Very high resource cost
- Blueprint investment required
- Non-destructive but highly effective control tool
