# Ships and Fleet Management

## Ship Classes and Customization

The game features a diverse range of ship classes, each with unique capabilities and module slots. For detailed statistics of each ship class, see [Ship Statistics](ship_stats.md).

### Ship Classes Overview

#### Combat Ships
- **Fighter**: Fast, agile combat vessels with high evasion but low durability. Perfect for hit-and-run tactics and escort missions.
- **Bomber**: Heavy attack vessels with increased durability but lower evasion. Specialized for dealing heavy damage to larger targets.
- **Destroyer**: Versatile combat vessels that balance firepower with maneuverability. Well-suited for various combat roles.
- **Cruiser**: Medium-sized combat vessels with significant firepower and armor. Excellent command vessels for smaller task forces.
- **Battleship**: Massive combat vessels with overwhelming firepower and heavy armor. Can carry a small complement of fighters.
- **Carrier**: Command vessels designed for fighter deployment and fleet coordination. Features extensive hangar facilities and powerful sensor arrays.

#### Specialist Ships
- **Scout**: Fast reconnaissance vessels with excellent scanning capabilities. Ideal for exploring new sectors and finding valuable resources.
- **Survey Ship**: Advanced reconnaissance vessels with superior scanning capabilities. Used for detailed resource surveying and long-range exploration.
- **Freighter**: Dedicated transport vessels with high cargo capacity. Essential for resource transportation between colonies.
- **Coloniser**: Specialized vessels designed for establishing new colonies. Once deployed, they transform into the colony's initial base building.

### Ship Customization
- Design ships by selecting modules for available slots
- Weapon modules range from rapid-fire lasers to devastating bombs
- Utility modules enhance ship capabilities (shields, engines, sensors, etc.)
- Each ship class has unique module compatibility restrictions

## Fleet Management
- Control and expand your fleet of mining vessels and military ships
- Manage ship maintenance and repairs
- Coordinate fleet movements and combat operations
- Balance fleet composition for different mission types
- Consider ship roles and capabilities when planning fleet composition
- Monitor maintenance costs and resource requirements
- Plan for crew requirements and training
- Coordinate carrier-based fighter operations
- Balance capital ships with support vessels
